## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are prospective business partners, job candidates, and the public who need to understand Ocean Space quickly on mobile and desktop. They are scanning for clarity: what the group is, how the business units relate, where to contact the team, and which career opportunities are relevant. The interface needs to help them grasp structure fast without feeling cold or confusing.

### Brand Personality
Disciplined, trustworthy, and operationally clear. The site should feel confident, structured, and precise rather than playful or flashy. Motion should reinforce order and responsiveness, with a small amount of delight that makes the interface feel polished instead of static.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode corporate/editorial with system-diagram cues, sharp information hierarchy, and restrained blue accents. The interface should feel mobile-first, airy, and readable, with movement that reveals relationships and state changes rather than drawing attention to itself. Avoid gimmicky flourishes, heavy parallax, or decorative motion that fights the content.

### Design Principles
1. Clarity before spectacle: motion should explain hierarchy, transitions, and feedback.
2. One calm rhythm: entrances, reveals, and hover states should share the same easing family and timing logic.
3. Mobile-first polish: animations must stay lightweight and readable on phone screens first.
4. Structural confidence: cards, sections, and calls to action should feel responsive and intentional.
5. Accessible by default: reduced motion must be respected and interactions must never block progress.

### Performance Constraints
- Static HTML with Tailwind CSS and vanilla JavaScript only.
- Mobile-first and performance-sensitive; use transform and opacity rather than layout animations.
- No heavy animation libraries.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with graceful non-animated alternatives.
